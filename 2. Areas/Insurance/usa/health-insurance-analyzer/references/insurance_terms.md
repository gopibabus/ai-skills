# Health Insurance Terms Reference

Quick reference for common insurance terminology to help with document analysis.

## Financial Terms

**Premium**
- Amount paid (usually monthly) to maintain insurance coverage
- Paid regardless of whether you use healthcare services
- Not included in deductible or out-of-pocket maximum

**Deductible**
- Amount you must pay out-of-pocket before insurance starts paying
- Typically resets annually (January 1)
- Separate amounts for individual vs family coverage
- Separate amounts for in-network vs out-of-network
- Preventive care usually exempt from deductible

**Out-of-Pocket Maximum (OOP Max)**
- Maximum amount you'll pay in a year for covered services
- Includes: deductible, copays, coinsurance
- Does NOT include: premiums, out-of-network care (in some plans), non-covered services
- Once reached, insurance pays 100% for covered services
- Resets annually

**Copay (Copayment)**
- Fixed amount you pay for specific services
- Example: $25 for primary care visit, $50 for specialist
- Usually due at time of service
- Typically does NOT count toward deductible
- DOES count toward out-of-pocket maximum

**Coinsurance**
- Percentage of costs you pay after meeting deductible
- Example: 20% coinsurance means you pay 20%, insurance pays 80%
- Applied to "allowed amount" or "negotiated rate"
- Counts toward out-of-pocket maximum

**Allowed Amount / Negotiated Rate**
- Maximum amount insurance will pay for a service
- What insurance company has negotiated with provider
- Your coinsurance is based on this amount, not provider's full charge

## Plan Types

**HMO (Health Maintenance Organization)**
- Must use in-network providers (except emergencies)
- Requires PCP (Primary Care Physician) selection
- Needs referrals to see specialists
- Lower premiums, less flexibility
- No out-of-network coverage (except emergency)

**PPO (Preferred Provider Organization)**
- Can see any provider, in or out of network
- No referrals needed for specialists
- Higher premiums, more flexibility
- Lower costs for in-network providers
- Some coverage for out-of-network (higher costs)

**EPO (Exclusive Provider Organization)**
- Must use in-network providers (except emergencies)
- No referrals needed for specialists
- No out-of-network coverage (except emergency)
- Middle ground between HMO and PPO

**POS (Point of Service)**
- Hybrid of HMO and PPO
- Lower costs with PCP and referrals (HMO-style)
- Option to go out-of-network (PPO-style) at higher cost

**HDHP (High Deductible Health Plan)**
- High deductible, low premium
- Often paired with HSA (Health Savings Account)
- Designed for healthy individuals or those who want to save in HSA
- Preventive care still covered at 100%

## Coverage Terms

**In-Network**
- Providers contracted with your insurance company
- Lower out-of-pocket costs
- Claims processed automatically
- Negotiated rates apply

**Out-of-Network**
- Providers not contracted with your insurance
- Higher out-of-pocket costs or no coverage
- May need to file claims yourself
- May face "balance billing"

**Balance Billing**
- When out-of-network provider bills you for difference between their charge and what insurance pays
- Example: Doctor charges $200, insurance allows $100, you pay deductible/coinsurance on $100, doctor bills you extra $100
- Illegal for in-network providers
- Protections exist for emergency care (No Surprises Act)

**Prior Authorization / Pre-Authorization**
- Insurance approval required BEFORE receiving certain services
- Common for: expensive medications, surgery, imaging, specialty treatments
- Failure to get authorization can result in denial of coverage

**Formulary**
- List of prescription drugs covered by plan
- Organized into tiers (1-4 or more)
- Tier 1: Generic (lowest cost)
- Tier 2: Preferred brand
- Tier 3: Non-preferred brand
- Tier 4: Specialty drugs (highest cost)

**Preventive Care**
- Services designed to prevent illness
- Covered at 100% (no cost-sharing) under ACA
- Includes: annual physical, screenings, immunizations
- Must be from in-network provider
- Diagnostic follow-up may not be free

**Medically Necessary**
- Services/treatments required to diagnose or treat an illness
- Insurance determines what's medically necessary
- Cosmetic procedures typically NOT medically necessary
- Can be appealed if denied

## Other Important Terms

**Explanation of Benefits (EOB)**
- Statement from insurance showing what was billed, what insurance paid, what you owe
- NOT a bill (bill comes from provider)
- Important to review for accuracy

**Claims**
- Request for payment submitted to insurance
- Usually submitted by provider (in-network)
- You may need to submit for out-of-network

**Coordination of Benefits (COB)**
- When covered by multiple insurance plans
- Determines which plan pays first (primary) and second (secondary)

**COBRA**
- Temporary continuation of employer health insurance after leaving job
- You pay full premium (including employer's share) plus admin fee
- Available for 18-36 months depending on qualifying event

**Qualifying Life Event (QLE)**
- Event allowing enrollment outside Open Enrollment
- Examples: marriage, birth, loss of coverage, moving
- Usually 60 days to enroll after event

**Open Enrollment**
- Annual period to enroll in or change health insurance
- Typically November 1 - December 15 for ACA marketplace
- Employer plans may have different dates

**Network**
- Group of providers contracted with insurance
- Narrow network = fewer providers, lower premium
- Broad network = more providers, higher premium

**Referral**
- Approval from PCP to see specialist
- Required in HMO and some POS plans
- Not required in PPO or EPO

**Step Therapy**
- Requirement to try lower-cost treatment before insurance covers expensive option
- Common for prescription drugs
- Can be appealed with medical justification

## Prescription Drug Terms

**Generic**
- Non-brand name version of drug
- Same active ingredients as brand
- Much lower cost
- Tier 1 in most formularies

**Brand Name**
- Original manufacturer's version
- Higher cost
- May be preferred or non-preferred on formulary

**Specialty Drugs**
- High-cost medications (often $500+/month)
- Usually for complex conditions
- May require special handling or administration
- Often highest tier (Tier 4 or 5)

**Mail Order**
- Prescription sent by mail for 90-day supply
- Usually cheaper than retail pharmacy
- Good for maintenance medications

**Prior Authorization (Rx)**
- Insurance approval needed before certain drugs covered
- Common for expensive or specialty drugs
- Doctor must provide justification

## Acronyms

- **ACA**: Affordable Care Act
- **COB**: Coordination of Benefits
- **DME**: Durable Medical Equipment
- **EOB**: Explanation of Benefits
- **ER**: Emergency Room
- **FSA**: Flexible Spending Account
- **HSA**: Health Savings Account
- **OOP**: Out-of-Pocket
- **OTC**: Over-the-Counter
- **PCP**: Primary Care Physician
- **Rx**: Prescription
- **UCR**: Usual, Customary, and Reasonable (rate)
